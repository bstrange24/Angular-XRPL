Payment Channel Constraints
Payment Channel Constraints
Description
Channel Creator Locks Funds
The sender (creator) funds the channel upfront with an Amount of XRP that is locked until claimed or returned.
One Receiver Only
Each channel has a fixed `Destination` (receiver). Only they can claim funds from it.
Receiver Must Submit Claims
The receiver presents **signed claims** from the sender to receive funds using `PaymentChannelClaim`
Only the Receiver Can Claim XRP
Only the `Destination` account may redeem claims — sender cannot reclaim incrementally.
Claims Must Be Signed By Sender
Claims must include a **valid signature from the sender** of the channel (proof of authorization).
Claims Must Increase
Each claim must specify an `amount` that is **greater than any previous** claimed amount — no partial duplicate claims.
Channel Expires (Optionally)
`CancelAfter` can be set. After that time, the sender can **close the channel** if there are unclaimed funds.
Close After Expiry or Fully Claimed
The sender or receiver can **close the channel** (via `PaymentChannelClaim` with `close=true`) once it's **expired or depleted
Renewable Before Expiry
If `CancelAfter` hasn't passed, the sender can top up the channel with `PaymentChannelFund` and extend its lifespan (with `Expiration`).
Signature Must Be Exact Format
The claim's signature must use the expected format with correct message digest structure (e.g., `channel_id`, `amount`, and canonical signature).
Amount in Claims is Cumulative
The `amount` in a claim is the **total authorized amount to date** — not an incremental delta.
No IOUs, XRP only
Payment Channels work with **XRP only** — not with issued tokens (IOUs).
